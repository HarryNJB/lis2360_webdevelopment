Giannis Antetokounmpo

Giannis dominated the league this year on both sides of the floor. His length and touch around the basket made him nearly impossible to guard. Pairing that with his developing 3-pt shot should make him a perennial MVP contender for many years to come.

James Harden

James Harden has developed into one of the most dynamic scorers in NBA history. His ability to finish from anywhere on the floor and his great passing out of the pick & roll have made him nearly unguardable without help. Harden led his team for a very poor start to a fantastic finishing record and the 4th seed in the Western Conference.

Paul George

Paul George performed at an MVP level in his first year of his new contract with the OKC Thunder. George was a pest on both sides of the floor and shot the 3-ball very well. While the Thunder's season came to a disappointing end in the first round of the playoffs, George carried the team as far as he could. His efficiency did not go unnoticed.

Giannis Antetokounmpo. Developing Supersstar

If Giannis Antetokounmpo develops a 3-point shot, then the league is doomed. He is already dominating everywhere else on the floor, racking up an impressive 27.7 ppg this season as well as being one of the league's best defenders. He has the best chance of being voted MVP.

James Harden. Best isolation scorer in NBA history.

James Harden's talent in passing and scoring has made him unguardable. He has the fastest first step in the league and blows by defenders with ease. Harden averaged 36.1 ppg during the regualr season, which is down right ridiculous. Harden is ahving one of the best statistical years in NBA history.

And lastly, Paul George. Unlucky.

George is unlucky because his competition for MVP are players having two of the craziest seasons in NBA history. Harden's scoring and Antetokounmpo's all around basketball have been revolutionary for the league. At another time, Paul George's stats would make him a clear cut MVP. But this year is not his year.
